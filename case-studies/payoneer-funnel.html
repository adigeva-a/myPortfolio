Background & Context

The opportunity hiding in plain sight

Payoneer's registration flow had grown organically over years ‚Äî compliance requirements layered on top of legacy UI, ambiguous field labels, no progress visibility, and session timeouts that wiped user progress. For a global payments platform serving freelancers, agencies, and businesses across 200+ countries, every drop-off had direct revenue impact.

When I took ownership of the registration product, the first thing I did was sit with the data. What I found was a funnel bleeding users at multiple stages ‚Äî and no shared understanding of why.

Problem Statement

What the data said

Funnel analysis revealed that less than 40% of users who started registration completed it. Drop-off was distributed across the funnel ‚Äî no single catastrophic failure, but death by a thousand cuts.

The business impact was significant: every percentage point of completion lift translated to thousands more activated accounts monthly. The problem was universally understood but had never been systematically tackled.

Research Conducted

A mixed-method investigation

I ran a parallel discovery track ‚Äî quantitative to understand where and how much, qualitative to understand why. Six months of Mixpanel funnel data across 14 countries gave me drop-off rates at each stage. FullStory session replays revealed hesitation, re-reads, and repeated failed field inputs.

Exit intent surveys captured intent signals from abandoning users. And 28 user interviews across freelancer and SMB segments surfaced recurring mental model mismatches ‚Äî the biggest being that "business type" selection was interpreted completely differently by different user personas.

Process

Key Learnings

What this taught me

  • Compliance-driven fields need context ‚Äî users comply when they understand why. "We need this to verify your identity" outperforms unlabeled form fields every time.
  • Progress visibility is a psychological lever. Showing "Step 2 of 5" reduces uncertainty and increases commitment to completing.
  • Surfacing document requirements early prevents the biggest late-funnel abandonment spike ‚Äî surprise is the enemy of completion.
  • Quick wins (copy changes, tooltip additions, reordering steps) often outperform large redesigns in speed-to-impact.
  • Support tickets are a goldmine ‚Äî the #1 registration complaint in tickets is almost always the #1 funnel drop-off cause.
  • Segmenting by user type (freelancer vs. SMB) revealed completely different mental models ‚Äî one flow can't optimally serve both without adaptation.
  • Session persistence is underrated. A "resume your application" email re-engaged 11% of abandoned users who became full completers.
